I am a mathematician specializing in dynamics, and I currently work as a postdoc at the University of Utah. Here my postdoc supervisor is Prof. Kurt Vinhage. I received my PhD from Penn State in May 2023 under the supervision of Prof. Federico Rodriguez Hertz. I hold a B.Sc. in mathematics and B.A. in philosophy from Koc University in Istanbul, Turkey.

I consider teaching mathematics to be my craft, and while I have been primarily a research mathematician so far, my long-term career goal is to transition to a position centered on teaching and guiding undergraduate research. I have extensive and diverse experience in teaching as well as mentoring student research.

My current research focus is smooth actions of compactly generated abelian Lie groups with some hyperbolicity. I am also interested in higher rank actions at large, as well as other dynamical areas like thermodynamic formalism, ergodic optimization and subriemannian dynamics.

In terms of philosophy I am interested in the problem of time, as well as the development of what I like to provisionally call dynamical phenomenology, a framework adjacent to Husserlian genetic phenomenology.
